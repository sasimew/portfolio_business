Insights

HOW I THINK

The strongest strategy work usually comes from combining structure, market sense, and a realistic understanding of what a team can actually execute.

1

BUSINESS MODEL FIRST

Advice should start with how value is created, captured, and defended. If the economic engine is unclear, everything else becomes fragile.

2

EXECUTION IS PART OF STRATEGY

A good strategy is not just conceptually strong. It has to survive timelines, internal capability, market constraints, and investor scrutiny.

3

CLARITY CREATES SPEED

When leaders can see tradeoffs clearly, teams move faster. The goal is to reduce ambiguity and increase confidence in the next move.

Platform Strategy

Designing a scalable marketplace model from early concept into commercial traction

A digital platform needed a strategy to reduce transaction friction, improve trust, and create a business model that could scale without breaking its economics.

Approach

  • Mapped supply and demand imbalances across the customer journey.
  • Defined monetization layers, take-rate logic, and trust mechanics.
  • Built KPIs for conversion, repeat usage, and value capture.

Impact

  • Created a clearer commercial model and growth roadmap.
  • Improved business confidence around how the platform should scale.
  • Turned abstract ambition into an executable structure.
02
Game Economy

Optimizing monetization and retention logic for a growing game ecosystem

A game business needed a more structured way to connect player behavior, live-ops design, pricing, and long-term revenue quality.

Approach

  • Segmented users by spending behavior and retention patterns.
  • Reframed pricing and offer architecture around ARPPU logic.
  • Designed engagement loops that linked community activity to monetization.

Impact

  • Improved strategic clarity on where monetization should come from.
  • Built a stronger retention narrative for product and business teams.
  • Created a more repeatable live-ops growth playbook.
03
Tokenomics

Designing a token model and fundraising narrative for market entry

A Web3 ecosystem needed a token structure that balanced growth incentives, stakeholder alignment, and long-term sustainability.

Approach

  • Defined token utility across users, investors, and platform activity.
  • Structured supply, rewards, vesting, and incentive mechanics.
  • Connected the token design to launch positioning and investor messaging.

Impact

  • Improved incentive alignment across key stakeholders.
  • Made the fundraising story more coherent and defensible.
  • Built a stronger foundation for long-term ecosystem health.
04
Valuation & Deal Strategy

Building investor-ready valuation logic for strategic planning and deal conversations

A growing digital business needed a structured financial story that could support fundraising, internal decisions, and external investor communication.

Approach

  • Built scenario-based revenue, cash flow, and valuation logic.
  • Translated assumptions into a decision-making framework.
  • Connected financial modeling to market narrative and strategic options.

Impact

  • Improved clarity in investor and leadership conversations.
  • Made tradeoffs easier to evaluate under uncertainty.
  • Strengthened the business case behind future strategic moves.
