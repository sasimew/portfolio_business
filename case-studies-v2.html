Scaling a Game Marketplace to THB 66M in 3 Months

Investment Thesis Growth was constrained not by traffic, but by misaligned monetization mechanics.

A newly launched gaming platform faced unclear monetization structure and weak early traction despite strong demand signals. High traffic existed, but conversion to revenue was structurally broken: the platform was acquiring users without a mechanism to capture value.

Key Issues
  • Weak monetization architecture
  • Low conversion despite strong traffic
  • No repeat purchase or retention loop
Strategic Actions
  • Redesigned pricing and monetization architecture
  • Built transaction-driven growth loop
  • Optimized conversion funnel and payment flow
  • Aligned product, marketing, and partnerships
Measured Outcomes
THB 66MRevenue in 3 months
299KPeak concurrent users
38%Monthly retention rate
Value Creation Insight

Revenue growth was driven by monetization alignment, not user acquisition. Fixing the conversion architecture unlocked value that already existed in the user base.

Structuring Southeast Asia Esports Strategy for Market Expansion

Investment Thesis Success in emerging markets depends on ecosystem orchestration, not just content distribution.

A global publisher required a localized strategy to enter fragmented SEA esports markets. The challenge was not product quality, but the absence of a coherent local ecosystem model against the realities of regional diversity.

Key Issues
  • Fragmented regional esports ecosystem
  • Weak local audience engagement model
  • Misalignment between global and local strategy
Strategic Actions
  • Designed localized esports market strategy
  • Structured partnership and engagement model
  • Aligned global positioning with local execution
Measured Outcomes
  • Scalable regional engagement framework
  • Strengthened local market positioning
  • Replicable model across SEA markets
Value Creation Insight

Local ecosystem control is the critical variable in emerging market entry. Distribution without community infrastructure creates reach without retention.

Investment Thesis Strategic events act as ecosystem catalysts when tied to platform growth, not standalone activations.

  • Requirement for measurable engagement impact
Strategic Actions

  • Delivered large-scale cross-border execution
Measured Outcomes

  • Operational playbook for future cross-border events

Events create value when integrated into growth strategy: not as brand spend, but as distribution infrastructure.

Building Strategic Partnerships with AMD and AirAsia

Investment Thesis Cross-industry partnerships unlock distribution channels and brand credibility that organic growth cannot replicate at speed.

Growth beyond the core gaming ecosystem required access to new audiences and distribution infrastructure. Partnerships with AMD and AirAsia were identified as high-leverage paths for credibility, travel-commerce integration, and reach expansion.

Key Issues
  • Limited reach beyond core gaming audience
  • Absence of strategic cross-industry partnerships
  • Untapped brand leverage and credibility opportunities
Strategic Actions
  • Initiated and structured partnership agreements
  • Aligned commercial incentives across industries
  • Integrated partnerships into core growth engine
Measured Outcomes
  • Secured global partnerships with AMD and AirAsia
  • Expanded ecosystem reach beyond core market
  • Established repeatable partnership framework
Value Creation Insight

Partnerships must be embedded into the growth engine. Standalone partnerships create moments; integrated partnerships create compounding distribution.
