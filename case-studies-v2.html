Case Study #1 Digital Platform · Gaming & Digital Goods

Scaling a Digital Platform to THB 250M+ GMV Run-Rate

Overview

A digital platform in the gaming and digital goods sector faced stalled growth despite strong early traction. Within 6 months, the platform transitioned from fragmented growth to structured scale, achieving sustainable unit economics and market-leading performance.

Problem
  • Stalled Growth: Platform hit plateau despite early momentum
  • Low Transaction Efficiency: High MAU but poor monetization conversion
  • Fragmented Liquidity: Disconnected supply-side ecosystem
  • Weak Pricing Discipline: Inconsistent pricing across sellers
  • CAC Dependency: Over-reliance on paid acquisition
  • No Unit Economics Framework: Growth without profitability path
Objective

Rebuild the platform into a scalable, economically sustainable marketplace by increasing monetization efficiency, strengthening supply-demand matching, reducing dependency on paid growth, and establishing clear unit economics.

Strategic Approach
1
Unit Economics ResetDefined transaction rate, ARPPU, and take rate targets with structured KPI tracking.
2
Liquidity & Supply OptimizationConsolidated sellers, prioritized high-conversion SKUs, introduced pricing discipline.
3
Conversion Funnel OptimizationOptimized discovery to payment flow, reduced friction, improved trust signals.
4
Monetization Layer ExpansionTiered pricing structure, optimized take rates, focused on high-value segments.
5
Growth RebalancingShifted from paid-heavy to organic + retention-led growth model.
Key Insight "Scale without unit economics is not growth -- it's delayed failure."

The inflection point came from prioritizing monetization efficiency over user volume, building liquidity before features, and treating pricing as a strategic lever.

Ecosystem Challenge

A Web3/GameFi project required a token model capable of supporting capital raise while sustaining long-term ecosystem health. Typical failure patterns include over-supply and inflation, no real utility, misaligned incentives, and short-term speculation.

Token Utility Design
  • In-platform transaction currency
  • Fee mechanism integration
  • Reward system alignment
Supply & Vesting Strategy
  • Structured vesting: Team, Investors, Ecosystem
  • Controlled emission rate
  • Long-term alignment mechanisms
Demand Mechanism
Staking ModelLock-up incentives for long-term holders
Fee Usage LoopContinuous utility demand generation
Buyback LogicLinked to platform revenue
Measurable Outcomes
  • $8M raised within 24 hours of launch
  • Strong investor demand across multiple rounds
  • High participation rate from target investors
  • Sustainable economic model established
Key Insight "Token price is not the product -- the economy is."

Success came from designing a sustainable economic system rather than optimizing for short-term price action. The token model enabled long-term ecosystem growth and built lasting investor confidence.

Investment Thesis Growth was constrained not by traffic, but by misaligned monetization mechanics.

A newly launched gaming platform faced unclear monetization structure and weak early traction despite strong demand signals. High traffic existed, but conversion to revenue was structurally broken: the platform was acquiring users without a mechanism to capture value.

Key Issues
  • Weak monetization architecture
  • Low conversion despite strong traffic
  • No repeat purchase or retention loop
Strategic Actions
  • Redesigned pricing and monetization architecture
  • Built transaction-driven growth loop
  • Optimized conversion funnel and payment flow
  • Aligned product, marketing, and partnerships
Measured Outcomes
